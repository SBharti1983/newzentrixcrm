Dropshipping is an eCommerce business model where you sell products without keeping inventory.



Instead of stocking products yourself, you forward customer orders to a supplier, who ships the product directly to the customer.



How dropshipping works

Customer

&#x20;   │

&#x20;   ▼

Places an order on your website

&#x20;   │

&#x20;   ▼

You receive the payment

&#x20;   │

&#x20;   ▼

You send the order to your supplier

&#x20;   │

&#x20;   ▼

Supplier packs and ships the product

&#x20;   │

&#x20;   ▼

Customer receives the order

Example



Imagine you sell a backpack.



You list it on your website for ₹1,499.

A customer buys it.

You pay your supplier ₹900.

The supplier ships it directly to the customer.

Your gross profit is ₹599 (before expenses like advertising and payment fees).

Advantages

✅ Very low startup cost

✅ No warehouse required

✅ No need to buy inventory upfront

✅ Easy to test new products

✅ Can be run from anywhere with internet access

Disadvantages

❌ Lower profit margins than holding your own inventory

❌ Less control over product quality

❌ Shipping delays can affect customer satisfaction

❌ Returns and refunds can be more complicated

❌ You're dependent on your suppliers

Typical dropshipping workflow

Create an online store.

Add products from a supplier.

Market your store (social media, ads, SEO, etc.).

Receive customer orders.

Send the orders to the supplier automatically or manually.

Supplier ships the products.

Provide customer support.
